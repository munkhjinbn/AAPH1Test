Safety, Approach, Departure, and Emergencies

Safety Basics

  • Always have ID card, ID tags, and earplugs.
  • Helmet on; chinstrap fastened with no twists.
  • Weapons: muzzle down, no round chambered, selector on safe.
  • Sleeves down to protect from debris and flash fires.
  • Secure loose gear IAW SOP.
  • Stay away from main rotor, tail rotor, cargo hook, and static electricity.
  • Seatbelt always fastened.

MEDEVAC Loading / Memory Notes

  • First MEDEVAC helicopter: OH-13 Sioux.
  • OH-13 disadvantages: no in-flight care, exposed to weather/temperature, exposed to enemy fire.
  • Loading sequence: ambulatory loaded first or last; litters loaded in Z pattern; most seriously injured loaded last.
  • CH-47 medic ratio: 1 medic per 6 patients.
  • CH-47 litter/ambulatory combos: 0/31, 4/25, 8/19, 12/16 normal, 16/10, 20/4, 24/1.

Marking / Controlling

  • Phases: selecting, marking, controlling.
  • Obstacles: anything 18 in high/wide/deep. Use 4 R’s: remove, reduce, mark red, radio.
  • Ground slope: 0–7° all aircraft; 8–15° cargo and medium utility can land with advisory.
  • Slope formula: vertical distance × 57.3 / horizontal distance.
  • Approach/departure: obstacle buffer 10:1 reducible to 5:1; no crosswind over 9 kts; no tailwind over 5 kts.
  • HAT = humidity, altitude, temperature.
  • Nine NATO formations: trail, staggered trail right, staggered trail left, echelon right, echelon left, heavy right, heavy left, vee, diamond. Diamond = most secure.

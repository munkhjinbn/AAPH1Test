Where do the three copies of DA Form 7382 go?
One copy to the aviation unit, one copy to the supported and one copy tied or taped on the load.
4 classifications of sling loads
certified, unique, prohibited, suitable
certified sling loads
items of equipment and their associated rigging procedures which have completed the evaluation and testing required for sling load certification
unique sling loads
These loads are equipment carried on a one time or low-frequency basis, such as telephone poles, artillery targets, or barrier material. The movement of unique loads should be approved by the high risk approving authority (O-6 or above).
prohibited sling loads
they have exhibited unstable flight characteristics and THEY DO NOT FLY
suitable sling loads
Not certified but have demonstrated acceptable static lift and flight characteristics during a flight test. NSC considers proven safe. Not certified but good to go
who is authorized to inspect a sling load
must be in the grade of E-4 or above and a graduate of any of the following: Pathfinder School, Sling Load Inspector Certification Course (SLICC), or Air Assault School
Lift Capacity Factors
wind, humidity, altitude, temperature, fuel
